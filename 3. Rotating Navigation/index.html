The Final Frontier

Jay Remmers

Alaska is refered to as the final frontier. Located to the northwest of Canada, Alaska is known as a vast wilderness of mountains and rivers, for its frigid winters, and its tough people. Most of Alaska's settlement came in the late 19th century due to gold prospecting in the Yukon. The indigious people have a vast influence of the life and nomenclature of the land. Over half of the population of lives in its 2 largest cities, Anchorage and Fairbanks, and 80% in its largest 5 boroughs. The United States has designated a vast about of area in Alaska to be protected lands. With 8 National Parks and 16 total nationally protected parks, i.e. monuments, preserves, rivers, historic parks, etc.

Alaska Range

Home to the tallest Mountain in North America, Mount Denali (The High One), and wilderness that isnt common place in much of the developed world, Alaska has become a big player in the tourism industry. Over 2 million people visit the state anually, nearly trippling the population. As the last great wilderness people flock north to experience nature as it was before civilization and urban-development. Alaska has beauty found year round, from the long summer days to the bright northern lights in the winter, Alaska has all to offer.
